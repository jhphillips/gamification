üéØ WHAT YOU LEARNED:

üì± When SMEs Are Late:

  • REACH OUT - Don't wait silently. A polite check-in shows professionalism.
  • OFFER TO RESCHEDULE - Respect their time. A rushed 15-min session is worse than a full rescheduled one.
  • PROTECT THE QUALITY - You're responsible for getting good content. That means having enough time.

üé§ When Audio Is Bad:

  • STOP IMMEDIATELY - Every minute of bad audio is wasted effort.
  • BE POLITE BUT DIRECT - "I'm noticing audio issues" is professional and non-blaming.
  • FIX BEFORE CONTINUING - Check headphones, mic settings, internet connection. Test before recording more.
  • YOU CAN'T FIX BAD AUDIO IN POST - This isn't Hollywood. Bad audio = re-record.

üí° When Lighting Is Bad:

  • FRAME IT AS "MAKING THEM LOOK GOOD" - "So you look great on camera" vs "your lighting is bad"
  • OFFER SOLUTIONS, NOT CRITICISM - "Would you be able to..." vs "You need to..."
  • BLAME TECHNOLOGY IF NEEDED - "Webex is having trouble..." gives them an out
  • YOUR JOB IS QUALITY - They'll thank you for making them look professional on video

üó£Ô∏è When SMEs Ramble:

  • REDIRECT WITH QUESTIONS - Specific questions = focused answers
  • ACKNOWLEDGE THEN REFOCUS - "That's fascinating! For this training, let's focus on..."
  • OFFER STRUCTURE - Give them 3 questions to answer. Creates natural boundaries.
  • TIME IS LIMITED - Your job is to get usable content, not record everything they know

üëî When They're Wrong (And Senior):

  • VERIFY, DON'T CHALLENGE - "Could you confirm..." vs "You're wrong"
  • BRING DOCUMENTATION - Facts > opinions. Have sources ready.
  • FOLLOW UP IN WRITING - Email gives them time to check without losing face on camera
  • ACCURACY > COMFORT - Wrong information in training = compliance risk. You MUST address it.

üìÖ When Schedules Won't Align:

  • STOP TRYING TO ALIGN EVERYONE - After 2-3 attempts, change approach
  • RECORD SEPARATELY, EDIT TOGETHER - Works perfectly and often looks more polished
  • PRIORITIZE BY SENIORITY - Schedule VPs first, others work around them
  • OFFER ASYNC OPTIONS - Loom/recorded video at their convenience. Underused but effective.

‚è±Ô∏è When Plans Change Mid-Recording:

  • ADAPT, DON'T CANCEL - Record who's available NOW, fix sequence in editing
  • OUT-OF-SEQUENCE IS FINE - Viewers don't know what order you recorded in
  • COMMUNICATE CHANGES - Brief SMEs when plans shift. Most people are flexible when informed.
  • PROTECT THE TIMELINE - Getting content today beats rescheduling for weeks

üîÑ When You Need Re-Records:

  • CATCH ERRORS BEFORE THEY LEAVE - Review with SMEs present. Fix immediately.
  • 30 SECONDS NOW > 3 DAYS LATER - Re-records are EASIER when caught immediately
  • EXPLAIN THE IMPACT - "This is a compliance issue" > "This is wrong"
  • TEXT CORRECTIONS LOOK UNPROFESSIONAL - Confuse learners. Fix properly with re-recording.

üèÉ When They're Talking Too Fast:

  • YOU CAN'T FIX PACING IN POST - Slowing down audio sounds robotic
  • REASSURE FIRST, REDIRECT SECOND - "You're doing great" before "Let's adjust"
  • TEACH TECHNIQUES - "Pause after each point" / "Imagine someone taking notes"
  • NERVOUS = FAST - Speed is usually anxiety. Address the anxiety, not just the symptom.

üò∞ When They Have First-Timer Jitters:

  • VALIDATE THE FEAR - "Everyone hates their own voice" is universal truth
  • TEST RECORDINGS BUILD CONFIDENCE - 30-second test, watch together, show them proof
  • OFFER AUDIO-ONLY OPTION - Just as effective, way less intimidating
  • PRACTICE RUNS WORK - Let them succeed without recording first. Confidence skyrockets.
  • THEY SOUND BETTER THAN THEY THINK - Always. Seeing proof breaks through fear.
